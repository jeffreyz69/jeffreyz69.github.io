This is a detailed view of the PC build.

Here is a basic desktop computer with decent parts. Not top of the line but plenty fast today to get many tasks done, from gaming to spreadsheets to video/photo editing with the price tag being around 1200. However you cannot carry a desktop everywhere so you also buy a laptop. Since you know you have a more capable main machine at home, you can decide to go for a mid range laptop that's plenty fast for tasks you most likely will do on the go, such as doing google docs or watching a video in another room, a m2 MacBook Air for 1000 is over kill but lets use it anyway for this example. The upfront cost is around 2200.

Here is a laptop for around the same price at 1100. In fact it has a slightly better* gpu than the desktop but comes with a screen and keyboard. The laptop like the desktop computer isn't top of the line but is plenty fast. So the upfront cost remains at 1100. But the laptop has a cpu, gpu, and ram that is not upgradable.

Lets say luck wasnt on your side and in two years time, right after the warranty expires, the gpu dies on both machines. On the desktop, you can buy the same component (a 3060 gpu) for a much lower price since it is now an older part. Or upgrade to a newer model for 400-500 depending on your new workflow such as playing more photorealistic games, the new overall cost for the desktop is now 2600.
Whereas for the laptop since the gpu is built right into the motherboard, replacing it is uneconomical so you decide to buy a new one. And if like the previous example decide you also now want to get into photorealistic gaming, a 1100 laptop would not be enough. You now have to get the 1500 one so now the overall cost for the laptops is 2600.

For a higher upfront cost and a little bit of research you know have a essential modern toolset that adapts to your needs. You don't have go out a buy an entire new machine when your workflow changes or when something breaks.
